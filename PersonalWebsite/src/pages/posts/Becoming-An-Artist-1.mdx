Becoming an artist is a dream by many people, a dream I share myself. But dreams are for your sleep not when you live.

*I want to become* a painter, a musician. Are big goals. Too big. When your goal becomes too big. Frustration becomes as big as the distance between you right now and your goal.

Climbing Mount Everest as your first summit will not work. But climbing a much smaller mountain seems much easier. It seems obvious not to give yourself too big goals.

But motivation is the biggest problem. When people have big goals those are mostly the biggest impact on motivation. They see a muscular body in social media and think, this is the body I dream of and start going to the gym.

How many people do you know that go to the gym for miniscule gains? They go for big gains. If they don't get it them fast enough they either quit or use steroids. It's this frustration that results in quitting or cheating.

It's easy to say, you have to give yourself small goals, keeping track of small task that are part of something bigger. But small goals give small motivation. For some It's not big enough to even pursue.

When I create my paintings I always want to create their best version, I painted so many ugly things, they broke my spirit. How could something beautiful emerge from that?

![A painting of two cats](/art/for_a_friend_her_cats.webp)

This is the finished version, before that I drew 3 hideous ones. I did it for her birthday, wouldn't it be for that I would have not finished it. And I would only have 3 hideous pieces to my name, that I were too ashamed to show.

This is **the struggle** to keep going even though everything in your mind just screams to stop and to do something else. To be motivated means to be able to sacrifice something. When you are not willing to sacrifice something I don't think you are motivated.

When people see that they need to sacrifice something they always think big. But the sacrifice is usually not *that* big. For example when painting or drawing the usual sacrifice is time. It simply takes much time to draw. All arts take time.

Would you sacrifice one hour of your day? I know people that not in a hundred years would do that.

While some people think a big sacrifice is money, the real sacrifice is your time. Because that is truly limited. And one day no time is left.

But I believe that this struggle imbues something in the art itself. To see something struggle but prevail is truly something motivating.

One major problem for many is that it does not matter how much you struggle, you won't get more rewards or better outcomes. Society as a whole does not reward the struggle.
