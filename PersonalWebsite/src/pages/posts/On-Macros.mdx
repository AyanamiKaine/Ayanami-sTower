Macros in Lisp are truly magical. They give you the ability to extend the language syntax. Directly embedding domain specific ideas and concepts inside your programming language.

But people hate them because when you know how to program in **Clojure** you can understand Clojure code, but you cannot understand the domain specific code. As it's effectively another programming language to learn with semantics and syntax.

And as we know people hate learning something new. Why should I learn something new when the old way was completely fine? It's just a big unnecessary abstraction or is it?.

## The Case For DSLs

I believe the ability to create higher level abstractions like a new syntax can become the key of success. Because we don't need to think in the lower level pieces anymore and can compose higher level abstractions instead.
